Within Subjects Designs

This design uses the same subject in two different conditions within the same experiement
If you have a subject participate in a design where they do set A and then set B. After test them on the same level to collect the results for analysis.
Example : have a subject do anaerobic activity then do a memory task and then do aerobic activity and do a different memory task of the same difficulty and compare the results
Having a high number of subjects and randomizing the order of the study should give significant results when it is time to analyze the data.

2x2 Between Subjects Factorial Design

While is most of our other designs we have looked at one independent variable, a 2x2 design presents another independent variable that is going to be tested.
Example : Taking two indepedent variables (temperature and difficulty of a test) to be tested against different levels of temperature (50F, 70F) and difficulty (easy, hard)

What would a 3x2 design look like?

In summary you would use qualitatively independent variables with different levels within the variables

2x2 Mixed Factorial Design

A combination of the first two designs, this design includes both between and within subjects. Each subject is given a pre-test and a post-test
Example: Depressed subjects are split into two groups of treatment. A baseline (pre-test) is given to both and then the treatment is done, followed by a post-test(experimental) to see the findings and results.
Discuss the pre-post-control design. One groups is the focus of the experiment (experimental) and the second is the base line(control).
Example : :This design would consist of one within subject variable (test), with two levels (pre and post), and one between subjects variable (therapy), with two levels (traditional and cognitive)

Latin Square Design

A between-subject design that has the subject see each item one time, see all conditions equally, and across lists each items should be seen equally.

Amazon Mechanical Turk: Online platform where researchers can post short experiments and tasks for workers (subjects) to complete

C

Comma Separated Value Format (CSV format): Plain text document format in which columns of data are separated by a comma (or other delimiter, such as a tab). Very computer-friendly. You can create one of these files by saving as csv in Excel.
Confound: Something in the experiment that might unintentionally affect the results. For example, to study how English speakers process a particular sentence structure in English, we may only want to run native, monolingual English speakers. Degree of nativeness or knowledge of another language might affect our results in unexpected ways and are potential confounds.
Control group: The group of subjects that serves as a point of comparison for the experimental group. For example, in a clinical trial to test the efficacy of a new drug, the experimental group would receive the drug while the control group might receive a sugar pill instead.
Control variable: Factors that are held constant in the experiment. For example, if we want to find out how sunlight affects plant growth, we would want to make sure all the plants receive the same amount of water and fertilizer, so that these factors will not affect our results.

D

Dependent Variable: The variable that responds to the change made in the independent variable; this is the quantity or value that is being measured. For example, to test how sunlight affects plant growth, a researcher might vary the amount of sunlight (independent variable) different plants receive. The growth of the plants is the dependent variable because it depends on the independent variable.

E

Eyetracker: Technology used to measure the position of where the subject is looking in real-time.

F

Filler: Items in the list that are used to make the subject unaware of what the experimenter is testing. E.g. If we are interested in reaction times to Sentence Structure A, we might also want to include some items that are of Sentence Structure B.

H

Hypothesis: What the researcher expects the results of the experiment to be

I

Independent Variable: The part of the experiment that is changed by the researcher. For example, to test how sunlight affects plant growth, a researcher might vary the amount of sunlight (independent variable) different plants receive. The growth of the plants is the dependent variable because it depends on the independent variable.

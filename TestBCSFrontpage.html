How Do I Create an Experiment?

The basics in helping you understand experimental design, terminology, and analysis with the ultimate goal in creating your own stimuli and experiment.

Start with Questions and a Hypothesis You Want to Explore

Have an interesting paper you read? Want to learn more on a topic? Just starting out and don't know where to look? Think about your interests within the class and topics you want to explore further. Why does this interest you? How will your project contribute to answering that question? What are ways you are going to implement your experiment? Start off with reading a paper or two, think about it critically and see what you would want to expand on or question. After you have a question you want to address, start brain storming a hypothesis to answer these questions.

I am a heading

A hypothesis is a prediction about how your variables interact with one another. A question simply asks what the interaction would be between the two variables.

Hypothesis: I predict reading times will be faster in condition A than condition B.
Question: What will the reading times be for condition A vs condition B?

When thinking of a hypothesis you want to identify the variables you want to test.

Example: I predict that plants will grow better when they are played music. Variables in this are plants who are grown with music playing and plants who have no music playing.
